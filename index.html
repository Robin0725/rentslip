FAQ

  • Are landlords required to give rent receipts? It varies by state and country. Many places require a receipt on request, and several require one automatically for cash payments. Some have no requirement at all. Check your local rules — this tool helps you comply, but it isn't legal advice.
  • How do I save the receipt as a PDF? Click "Print / Save as PDF", then in your browser's print dialog choose "Save as PDF" as the destination instead of a printer. Works in Chrome, Edge, Safari and Firefox on desktop and most phones.
  • Is this legally valid? A receipt is valid because of what it says and who signed it, not which tool produced it. This generates a standard, complete receipt (parties, property, period, amount, date, signature line). For disputes or unusual situations, ask a local professional.
  • Can I make receipts for past months? Yes — every date field is editable, so you can issue a receipt for any period. Just make sure the dates reflect what actually happened.
  • Is my data stored anywhere? No. Everything runs in your browser and nothing is uploaded or saved. We have no analytics, no cookies, no server-side anything. If you refresh the page, the form resets.
  • Can I generate a whole year at once? Not yet — batch generation (12 months in one go) is the headline feature of RentSlip Pro, a $9 one-time purchase we're building. Until then, the "New receipt" button makes monthly repeats fast: it advances the period and receipt number for you.
